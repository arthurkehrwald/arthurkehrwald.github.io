Work Experience

TH Köln - My current project will be part of an upcoming exhibition on future technologies and the impact they could have on day to day life in a new branch of the Deutsches Museum in Nuremberg. It will allow visitors to explore a virtual reality version of the marketplace near the museum 50 years in the future. I work primarily as a shader programmer in a medium sized team of around 20 students and freelancers. Given the limited hardware capabilities of the Android based Oculus headsets, writing performant code has been essential. Apart from shader programming, I am in charge of dealing with the issues that arise from using a VR Headset built for a single user standing in a small room for a game that has four players walk around on 60 square meters. I've implemented a calibration system that aligns all headsets to the room in the same way to ensure a smooth experience without concussions.
